Royal Fish (Album)

Royal Fish
Released: August 28th, 2024
Genres: Rock, Psychedelic Rock, Indie Rock, Folk Rock, Jazz
Run Time: 44:21

Royal Fish is the band's self-titled debut album released on August 28th, 2024 (the first day of classes at the College of William & Mary). The album was released initially on YouTube, Soundcloud, and Bandcamp. On September 5th, 2024, the album was released on other streaming services, including Spotify and Apple Music. The album consists of 11 songs. The album's total runtime is 44 minutes and 21 seconds.

Album Cover

The album cover was painted by Emily on a 12x12 canvas. It is a realistic depiction of the Royal Fish mascot, King Fish. He is a European Sturgeon. The fish is dressed in a blue cape with gold fringe and wears a crown similar to that of the crown jewels of the United Kingdom. He is seated on a throne that mimics the English coronation chair, although the gilded lions at the bottom are replaced by the William & Mary griffin.

History

Emily was the first to conceptualize the album, and had the idea for the album cover before any of the songs. The name of the album was inspired by the band Black Sabbath's song, "Black Sabbath," on their first album, Black Sabbath. This gave rise to the idea of "Royal Fish.".
